Sustainability Through the Ages

Explore Intel's journey through time, discovering how our commitment to innovation has shaped a more sustainable future for technology and our planet.

1968

Intel Founded

Intel is founded in Silicon Valley, setting the stage for decades of semiconductor innovation.

Robert Noyce and Gordon Moore established Intel with a focus on semiconductor memory and logic technologies that would shape modern computing.

1971

First Microprocessor

Intel introduces the 4004, the world’s first commercial microprocessor.

By placing a CPU on a single chip, Intel transformed electronics, enabling smaller, faster, and more efficient computing devices.

1978

8086 Processor

The 8086 processor establishes the x86 architecture.

x86 became the foundation for personal computers and servers, powering much of today’s global computing infrastructure.

1985

386 Processor

Intel launches the first 32-bit processor for PCs.

The 386 enabled multitasking, advanced software, and greater memory access—pushing computing into a new era.

2006

Peak Emissions

Intel reaches peak operational greenhouse gas emissions.

This milestone marked a turning point, driving investments in renewable energy, chemical abatement, and efficiency improvements.

2020

RISE Strategy

Intel launches its RISE sustainability strategy.

RISE focuses on Responsible, Inclusive, Sustainable, and Enabling goals to drive industry-wide progress.

2022

Net-Zero Commitment

Intel commits to net-zero greenhouse gas emissions by 2040.

The commitment covers Scope 1 and 2 emissions across global operations through clean energy and efficiency initiatives.

2023

Renewable Electricity

Intel reaches 99% renewable electricity usage worldwide.

Renewable energy adoption significantly reduces Intel’s operational carbon footprint while supporting growing compute demand.

2024

Sustainability Summit

Intel hosts its first Sustainability Summit.

Industry leaders collaborate on next-generation sustainable semiconductor manufacturing and supply chain innovation.
